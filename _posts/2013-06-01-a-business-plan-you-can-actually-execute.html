--- layout: post title: A Business Plan You Can Actually Execute categories: [] tags: [] status: draft type: post published: false meta: Hide SexyBookmarks: '0' _edit_last: '1' Hide OgTags: '0' --- I had breakfast with Ric Calvillo this morning, and he offered a perspective I had never considered: that a business plan does not describe how the business should be run, but how the founders should run it. That is to say, the plan can (arguably, "should") be designed around the strengths and weaknesses of the founders. Let's see some examples. Lone engineer: Create a business plan that puts most of the value on the product since that is where the founder is strongest, and find a simple go-to-market strategy. Don't plan on hiring a large sales team—that will be cumbersome and difficult to do well. A good cofounder would be a great product marketer who can also sell.
